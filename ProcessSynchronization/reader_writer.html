About the Reader-Writer Problem

  • The Reader-Writer Problem is a classic synchronization problem in operating systems.
  • It involves a shared resource (e.g., a database) that can be accessed by multiple readers or a single writer at a time.
  • Readers can access the resource simultaneously without conflicts.
  • Writers require exclusive access to the resource to prevent data inconsistency.
  • The problem ensures that:
    • Readers do not block other readers.
    • Writers have exclusive access to the resource when writing.
    • Deadlocks and starvation are avoided.
  • This simulator demonstrates the behavior of readers and writers in a shared environment.
