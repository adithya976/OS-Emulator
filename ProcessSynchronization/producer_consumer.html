About the Producer-Consumer Problem

  • The Producer-Consumer Problem is a classic synchronization problem in operating systems.
  • It involves a shared buffer that is used by producers to store items and by consumers to retrieve items.
  • The problem ensures that:
    • Producers do not add items to the buffer when it is full.
    • Consumers do not remove items from the buffer when it is empty.
    • Producers and consumers operate concurrently without conflicts.
  • This simulator demonstrates the behavior of producers and consumers in a shared environment.
